Nicole Ramker About!

Originally from NE Iowa, Nicole is a third-year undergraduate at the University of Iowa pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Environmental Sciences on a Biosciences Track as well as a Geographic Information Systems (GIS) Certificate and Geoscience Minor. Throughout her time at Iowa, she has been involved in a variety of outdoor learning experiences such as field ecology classes at Iowa Lakeside Laboratory in Milford, IA and Geoscience Department field trips to Badlands National Park, South Dakota; San Salvador Island, The Bahamas; St. Francois Mountains, Missouri; and soon this fall, Porcupine Mountains Wilderness State Park, Michigan. Nicole is also the Living Labs intern at the University of Iowa’s Office of Sustainability and the Environment. In this position, she helps work towards the mission of establishing more greenspaces on Iowa’s campus to make outdoor learning and research more accessible. Some examples of the work that Nicole has done for her internship includes developing a survey about usage of campus greenspaces, assisting with website and social media content creation, and designing a picnic blanket check out program to get students outside. This summer, she will be doing undergraduate research at Ashton Prairie Living Laboratory. Outside of school, Nicole loves to hike, read, bike, drink coffee, travel, and listen to live music!
